Authentic Tourism in Chilean Patagonia

At Travesía Local, we design travel experiences in Tortel that blend culture, nature, and daily life. Our approach integrates navigation, landscape interpretation, and meaningful community encounters, allowing you to discover Chilean Patagonia from an authentic and intimate perspective.

Each activity is designed to connect visitors with the landscapes, history, and people who give this unique corner of southern Chile its identity. Small groups, personalized attention, and a sustainable approach are core parts of our identity.

Discover our experiences and choose how you want to experience Tortel.

Exploring the Tortel Boardwalks

Guided Tour of the Boardwalks and Local History

An interpretative tour along the iconic boardwalks of Tortel, an architectural masterpiece built entirely from Guaitecas cypress wood.

During the walk, you will learn about the history of the early settlers, traditional construction techniques, adaptation to the environment, and the lifestyle dynamics in one of the most singular villages in Chilean Patagonia.

The route includes natural viewpoints with privileged vistas of the fjord, as well as spaces for conversation to help you understand the cultural identity of the territory.

What Is Included:

  • Host fluent in Spanish, English, or French.
  • Snack featuring local products (vegetarian option available).
  • Waterproof rain ponchos in case of rain.

Duration: 1 hour and 15 minutes.

Group Size: Maximum of 5 people.

Ideal for first-time visitors to Tortel.

Book your guided walk along the boardwalks and discover the history from within.

Woodcarving Workshop

Cultural Experience and Traditional Crafts in Tortel

Immerse yourself in the timber tradition that shaped the origins of Tortel through a hands-on woodcarving workshop.

You will discover the tools, techniques, and heritage knowledge passed down through generations, gaining a deep understanding of the historical relationship between the community and the forest. Each participant will craft their own wooden spoon using local wood and traditional methods.

More than just a workshop, this is a meaningful opportunity to connect with traditional craftsmanship and experience the slow-paced rhythm of Patagonian life.

What Is Included:

  • Host fluent in Spanish, English, or French.
  • Snack featuring local products.
  • All necessary tools and materials provided.

Duration: 2 hours and 30 minutes.

Group Size: Maximum of 5 people.

An authentic experience designed for those seeking cultural tourism in Tortel.

Book your workshop and create your very own piece from native wood.

Bahía Viva (Living Bay)

Packrafting in the Baker River Delta and Birdwatching

A packrafting experience in Tortel, ideal for those seeking a smooth and contemplative navigation.

We paddle through the shortest delta of the Baker River, exploring a vibrant ecosystem where turquoise waters, evergreen forests, and migratory birds converge. Throughout the journey, we engage in environmental interpretation, local flora and fauna observation, and landscape reading.

The crossing includes a beach landing to enjoy a snack featuring local products before continuing toward Tortel Bay, entering the village from the water and appreciating its wooden walkways from a unique perspective.

What Is Included:

  • Host fluent in Spanish, English, or French.
  • Full packrafting and safety equipment.
  • Binoculars and magnifying glasses for observation.
  • Snack featuring local products.

Duration: 3 hours.

Group Size: Maximum of 4 people + guide.

Perfect for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts.

Book your Bahía Viva experience and discover Tortel from the water.

Baker River in Packraft

Descent Along Chile’s Highest-Volume River

An active river journey along the widest branch of the Baker River, the highest-volume river in Chile.

We paddle in packrafts through vibrant, deep turquoise waters, passing by the Cascada Pisagua stream and skirting the historic Isla de los Muertos—where an optional landing and guided interpretative tour are available for an additional cost.

Throughout the navigation, we share the natural and historical context of the territory, weaving together landscape, memory, and local culture. We will make a beach stop to rest and enjoy a snack featuring local products before continuing toward Tortel Bay, entering the village straight from the river.

What Is Included:

  • Host fluent in Spanish, English, or French.
  • Full packrafting and safety equipment.
  • Snack featuring local products.
  • Historical and natural interpretation.

Duration: 5 hours

Group Size: Maximum of 4 people + guide.

Recommended for active travelers and adventure enthusiasts.

Book your Baker River descent and experience Patagonia in motion.

Tardes Clandestinas (Clandestine Evenings)

Boutique Culinary Experiences in Tortel’s Natural Landscapes

An intimate experience that blends local gastronomy, breathtaking landscapes, and boat navigation in Tortel.

It could be a toast out on the water, a sunset gathering on the beach, or a homemade lunch by the fireside. Each gathering is uniquely designed based on the season and weather conditions, highlighting pristine natural settings and local ingredients.

More than just a meal, this is an opportunity to connect through conversation, explore the territory, and immerse yourself in authentic Patagonian hospitality.

What Is Included:

  • Host fluent in Spanish, English, or French.
  • Dinner or appetizer course featuring local products and beverages.
  • Boat navigation tailored to the chosen experience format.

Duration: 2 hours and 30 minutes.

Group Size: Maximum of 5 people.

Ideal for couples, special celebrations, or small groups.

Inquire about availability and experience a different kind of evening in Tortel.

Packraft Rental

Navigate Tortel Bay at Your Own Pace

Packraft rental service in Tortel to independently explore the bay and observe the boardwalks from the water.

This option allows you to discover the coastline, paddle alongside local boats, and contemplate the village's characteristic wooden architecture from a privileged perspective.

Before setting sail, we provide a safety briefing and route recommendations to ensure a responsible and secure experience.

What Is Included:

  • Packraft, paddle, life jacket, and dry bag.
  • Safety briefing and induction.
  • Navigation and route recommendations.

Duration: Approximately 1 hour.

Group Size: Maximum of 6 people.

Ideal for those seeking freedom and direct contact with the environment.

Book your packraft and explore Tortel Bay on your own.
